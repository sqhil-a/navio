Accessibility Statement

Access is part of the mission, not an afterthought.

Navio Pathways is committed to making its website, communications, and future activities more usable for people with disabilities and different access needs.

Last updated: July 14, 2026

Draft for organizational review: This document is a practical website draft, not legal advice. Navio Pathways must review and approve it before relying on it as a final policy.

Our commitment

We aim to follow WCAG 2.2 Level AA best practices where reasonably possible and to improve when barriers are identified. Accessibility includes digital design, communication, registration, activities, venues, and the way accommodation requests are handled.

Website practices

  • Semantic headings, landmarks, lists, links, and form controls
  • A skip link and full keyboard access
  • Visible keyboard focus indicators
  • Responsive layouts and touch targets designed for mobile use
  • Text and controls with accessible contrast
  • Labels, instructions, validation, and status messages for forms
  • Reduced-motion support
  • Useful alternative text for meaningful images and empty alt text for decorative images
  • No essential information communicated by colour alone

Accommodations for activities

Future event and program listings should explain known accessibility information and how to request an accommodation. Requests will be considered in good faith, but an accommodation is not confirmed until Navio Pathways responds.

Known limitations

The site may link to third-party services whose accessibility Navio Pathways does not control. Online form processing is not active until a real endpoint is configured; email remains available as an alternative contact method. If a resource or process is difficult to use, please tell us.

Feedback and alternate formats

Email hello@naviopathways.com with “Accessibility feedback” in the subject line. Describe the page or task, the barrier, your preferred way to receive information, and any time sensitivity. Do not include unnecessary sensitive medical information.

Review

This statement should be reviewed after major website changes, when a barrier is reported, and before launching a new form, event, or program.
