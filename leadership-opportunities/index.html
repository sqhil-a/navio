Leadership development

Leadership is something you practise, not only a title you receive.

Young people build leadership by noticing what a group needs, communicating clearly, including others, making responsible decisions, and following through.

Opportunity status: No active leadership cohort, executive role, or youth-led project recruitment is currently posted. Navio Pathways will publish defined roles with real responsibilities and commitments when they are approved.

Leadership in action

Six habits you can practise in ordinary situations.

Notice

Pay attention to the goal, the people affected, missing information, and barriers that others may not see.

Clarify

Help the group understand what success means, who is responsible, and what needs to happen next.

Include

Make room for perspectives that are quieter, different, or often overlooked.

Decide

Use available information, explain tradeoffs, and know when a decision needs adult or expert input.

Follow through

Complete commitments, communicate when plans change, and do the less-visible work that keeps a project moving.

Reflect

Ask what worked, what impact you had, what feedback you received, and what you will adjust next time.

Build evidence

Describe what you did, not only the position you held.

After a project, record the situation, your responsibility, the action you took, and what changed. Be accurate about your contribution and separate team outcomes from your individual role.

Reflection prompt

“The group needed ____. I was responsible for ____. I chose to ____. The result or lesson was ____.”

Reflect on your practice

Use questions that turn an experience into a useful lesson.
