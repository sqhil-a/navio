Code of Conduct and Youth Safety

Respectful spaces need clear expectations and clear ways to raise concerns.

This draft applies to future Navio Pathways events, programs, volunteer activity, mentorship, online spaces, and other organization-connected interactions.

Last updated: July 14, 2026

Draft for organizational review: This document is a practical website draft, not legal advice. NAVIO PATHWAYS must review and approve it before relying on it as a final policy.
Emergency note: Navio Pathways is not an emergency service. If someone is in immediate danger, contact local emergency services. Do not rely on a website form for an urgent response.

1. Who must follow this code

Participants, youth, volunteers, mentors, speakers, team members, leaders, contractors, guests, and partners are expected to follow this code when taking part in Navio Pathways-connected activity.

2. Respectful behaviour

  • Treat people with dignity across differences in identity, background, ability, experience, and viewpoint
  • Use names and communication preferences respectfully
  • Listen without intimidation, humiliation, or retaliation
  • Follow event, venue, platform, privacy, accessibility, and safety instructions
  • Keep commitments or communicate promptly when plans change

3. Harassment and harmful conduct

Harassment, discrimination, bullying, threats, stalking, sexualized conduct, unwanted contact, retaliation, exploitation, hazing, and deliberate exclusion are not acceptable. Possession or use of weapons, illegal substances, or behaviour that creates a safety risk is prohibited in organization-connected activity.

4. Adult-youth boundaries

Adults and older youth in positions of responsibility must keep communication appropriate, transparent, and connected to the approved activity. They must not seek secrecy, private dependency, romantic or sexual interaction, financial entanglement, or unnecessary one-to-one access. Personal transportation, gifts, photography, private messaging, physical contact, and meetings outside approved channels require clear organizational rules and, where appropriate, guardian consent.

5. Communication and privacy

Use approved channels and include a guardian or designated organizational contact when required. Do not share a young person’s personal information, images, story, schedule, location, or contact details without proper permission and a legitimate purpose. Never pressure someone to disclose personal experiences.

6. Events and online spaces

Participants must follow stated supervision, arrival, departure, attendance, recording, chat, moderation, and emergency procedures. Accessibility needs should be handled respectfully and privately. No one should arrange an unapproved private meeting through a Navio Pathways activity.

7. Reporting a concern

Concerns connected to Navio Pathways should be reported as soon as reasonably possible to hello@naviopathways.com with “Youth safety concern” in the subject line. Share what happened, when and where it occurred, who was involved, and any immediate safety need. Do not investigate the matter yourself or promise confidentiality you cannot guarantee.

8. Response and consequences

Navio Pathways may take steps to protect participants, preserve information, limit contact, remove someone from an activity, end a role, notify a guardian, venue, partner, platform, insurer, or appropriate authority, and document the response. Consequences will depend on the concern, available information, safety needs, and applicable requirements. Retaliation against someone who raises a good-faith concern is prohibited.

9. Approval required before launch

NAVIO PATHWAYS must designate a trained youth-safety contact, establish escalation and recordkeeping procedures, confirm screening rules, and obtain appropriate professional review before this draft is treated as an operating policy.
