Mentorship and professional exposure

A useful conversation can widen what a young person knows is possible.

Navio Pathways is developing structured ways for youth to learn from professionals while keeping expectations, privacy, boundaries, and safety clear.

For youthFor professionals
Current status: Navio Pathways does not currently advertise active mentor matching. An inquiry is not a match, placement, reference, internship, or job opportunity.

For youth

Prepare to learn, not to perform.

A mentor or professional can share perspective, ask useful questions, and point toward resources. They cannot decide your future, guarantee an opportunity, or replace the support of qualified professionals.

  • Research basic public information before the conversation
  • Prepare focused questions about tasks, choices, and lessons
  • Keep communication through approved channels
  • Do not share private information you do not need to share
  • Respect the person’s time and boundaries
  • Choose one action to take after the conversation

Three strong questions

  1. What surprised you when you entered this field?
  2. Which skill do beginners underestimate?
  3. What is one low-risk way I could test my interest?

For professionals

Share context without making promises.

Potential contributions may include a career talk, panel, resource review, workshop, or future structured mentorship role. Every format should have a defined purpose, audience, time commitment, and communication channel.

  • Use age-appropriate, inclusive language
  • Be honest about multiple routes and tradeoffs
  • Keep adult-youth boundaries transparent
  • Avoid recruiting, favouritism, or requests for private contact
  • Do not promise admission, internships, jobs, or outcomes
  • Follow screening, consent, privacy, and reporting procedures
Express professional interest

Safety first

Mentorship needs structure around the relationship.

Before any recurring mentor activity launches, Navio Pathways must confirm eligibility, screening, matching criteria, guardian involvement, approved communication, recordkeeping, supervision, reporting, accessibility, and how a relationship ends.
