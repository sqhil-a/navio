Partner with us

Bring useful people, experiences, and opportunities closer to young people.

Navio Pathways welcomes thoughtful conversations with schools, youth organizations, community groups, professionals, businesses, and sponsors whose goals align with youth development.

Start a partnership inquiryUnderstand our mission

Who we work with

Partnership begins with a clear shared benefit for youth.

Schools and educators

Explore workshops, speakers, resource sharing, referrals, or student participation that fits school policies and needs.

Youth and community organizations

Coordinate complementary programming, community events, volunteer pathways, or access to trusted local support.

Professionals and mentors

Share informed career perspective through structured talks, panels, resources, or future mentorship activity.

Businesses

Offer workplace insight, employee volunteers, project expertise, in-kind support, or appropriate youth exposure.

Sponsors and supporters

Discuss mission-aligned financial or in-kind support with transparent scope and recognition. No tax benefit is represented.

Youth opportunity providers

Share a genuine, safe, age-appropriate opportunity for review before it is communicated to young people.

Partnership models

Start with the need, then choose the format.

01

Guest speakers and career panels

Help youth understand how a field works, what entry paths look like, and what professionals wish they had known.

02

Workshops

Develop a practical session around career exploration, communication, leadership, networking, or workplace readiness.

03

Mentorship

Explore a structured, screened, and youth-safe approach to professional exposure or recurring guidance.

04

Volunteer placements

Share a legitimate role with clear duties, supervision, eligibility, safety, timing, and documentation.

05

Event collaboration

Co-plan a mission-aligned activity with defined responsibilities, accessibility needs, audience, and outcomes.

06

In-kind support or sponsorship

Discuss space, expertise, tools, promotion, or funding tied to a clear youth-development purpose.

What we will ask

A good inquiry makes the intended youth benefit specific.

  • Who you represent and your role
  • The problem or opportunity you want to address
  • The young people who may benefit
  • Your proposed contribution and requested support
  • Possible format, location, timing, and accessibility needs
  • Any eligibility, screening, consent, privacy, or safety considerations
  • How success could be assessed without inflated claims

What partnership does not imply

An inquiry does not create an endorsement, contract, sponsor status, volunteer placement, or program commitment. Names and logos are published only after permission and scope are confirmed.
